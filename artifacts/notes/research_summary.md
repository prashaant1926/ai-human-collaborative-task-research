## Theoretical Framework: Collaborative Intelligence Framework (CIF)

### Four Core Components:

1. **Dynamic Role Allocation** - Adaptive assignment of leadership/execution responsibilities based on real-time competency assessment
2. **Persistent Context Management** - Shared memory system maintaining research history, insights, and evolving hypotheses
3. **Metacognitive Coordination** - Explicit process monitoring and optimization through collaborative reflection
4. **Task-Specific Optimization** - Specialized collaboration patterns for different research activities

## Literature Review Findings

### Key Research Gaps Identified:
1. **Dynamic Role Allocation** - 90% of existing work uses fixed role assignments
2. **Long-term Studies** - Only 12% of papers study interactions >1 week
3. **Research-Specific Metrics** - General collaboration metrics miss domain-specific outcomes
4. **Individual Differences** - Limited personalization frameworks

### Critical Literature-Level Assumptions:
1. **"AI as Tool" Paradigm** - May limit collaborative potential
2. **"Fixed Capability" Model** - Ignores learning and adaptation over time
3. **"Task-Independent" Collaboration** - One-size-fits-all approaches may be suboptimal
